The Evolve of World GDP in the Past Decades

The GDP around the world has changed dramatically in the past few decades. Many countries witnessed huge economic growth and increased their GDP. However, the change depends on the countries policies and the region they are located in. The following graphs will explore the economic growth and GDP changes of the world in the past few decades.

GDP Per Country in The Past Decades

Total GDP Per Country and Total GDP Per Capita between 2000 and 2020

Different countries had different growth rates depending on their annual growth and the location of the country. As seen in the map above, East asian Countries have had the biggest increase in their GDP between 2000 and 2020 which is due to their industrial revolution. If we slide the year slider between 2000 and 2020, it will be noticed that the Chinese GDP increased from 1.2 trillion dollar to 14.7 trillion dollars in 2020. The Chinese GDP doubled more than 10 times in just 20 years. On the other side of the world, the western countries have witnessed a smaller growth in GDP. For instance, the UK GDP increased from 1.6 Trillion dollars in 2020 to 2.7 trillion dollars in 2020. So the UK GDP has not even doubled in 20 years. However, the western countries maintained their high GDP per capita which is a result of slow population growth. For instance, even though China witnessed a huge economic boost, the GDP per capita remained at 10 thousands USD as per 2020 while the GDP per capita in the United Kingdom is 41 thousand as of 2020.
